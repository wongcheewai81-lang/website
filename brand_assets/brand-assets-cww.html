05 Usage Rules
Approved Backgrounds
The logo is approved for use on white (#ffffff), cream (#faf7f2), and navy (#0f2340) backgrounds only. Use the light lockup on white and cream. Use the dark lockup on navy. On photography, ensure sufficient contrast beneath the mark.
Foveal Gold — Significance
The gold segment on the ILM arc represents the foveal depression — the anatomical site of highest visual acuity. Gold is reserved exclusively for this mark element, the logotype rule, and the tagline separator. Do not use gold as a general accent elsewhere in the system.
Minimum Size
The full lockup (mark + logotype) must not be reproduced smaller than 280px wide on screen or 74mm in print. At small sizes, use the isolated logomark only. The minimum logomark size is 24px height (ILM arc only variant).
Clear Space
Maintain clear space of ½ × the mark height around the complete lockup on all sides. Never place the logo against busy patterns, low-contrast photography, or coloured backgrounds outside the approved palette without specific approval.
Do Not
Do not rotate, skew, or distort the mark. Do not recolour the teal arcs. Do not use the logotype without the mark. Do not add drop shadows or effects. Do not alter the weight or tracking of the logotype. Do not use the logo on coloured backgrounds not in the approved palette.
Mark Orientation
The retinal cross-section mark is always oriented as a C shape opening to the right. This orientation is fixed — it represents light entering the eye from the vitreous (right/interior) side toward the retina, with the foveal gold at the centre of highest acuity.
