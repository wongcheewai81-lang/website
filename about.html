Clinical Expertise

Vitreoretinal Surgery

Dr Chee Wai Wong specialises in vitreoretinal surgery, a subspecialty of ophthalmology focused on treating diseases of the retina, macula, and vitreous. His clinical expertise encompasses the full range of complex retinal pathologies, from acute presentations requiring emergency intervention to chronic conditions requiring long-term management.

His approach combines technical surgical expertise with careful patient communication and individualised treatment planning, grounded in published research and the latest clinical evidence.

Conditions Treated

What Dr Wong Treats

Dr Chee Wai Wong provides specialist care for a wide range of retinal and vitreous conditions.

Macular Conditions

Age-Related Macular Degeneration (AMD)

Treatment of wet and dry macular degeneration, including anti-VEGF therapy and geographic atrophy management.

Macular Holes

Full-thickness and lamellar macular hole repair using vitrectomy with internal limiting membrane peeling.

High Myopia Complications

Myopic macular degeneration, myopic CNV, and structural complications in highly myopic eyes.

Myopic Traction Maculopathy

Management of foveoschisis and retinoschisis in highly myopic eyes.

Diabetic Macular Oedema

Treatment of macular swelling associated with diabetes using anti-VEGF and steroid injections.

Epiretinal Membranes

Surgical removal of scar tissue on the retinal surface causing visual distortion.

Retinal Conditions

Retinal Detachment

Rhegmatogenous and tractional retinal detachment repair. A surgical emergency — Dr Wong offers urgent assessment and intervention.

Diabetic Retinopathy

Advanced diabetic eye disease management including laser, anti-VEGF, and vitreous surgery.

Retinal Vein Occlusion

Central and branch retinal vein occlusion management with anti-VEGF and laser treatment.

Eye Floaters & Vitreous Conditions

Assessment and management of posterior vitreous detachment, symptomatic floaters, and vitreous haemorrhage.

IOL-Related Conditions

Dislocated Intraocular Lens

Management of IOL decentration and late dislocation following cataract surgery.

IOL Exchange & Scleral Fixation

Replacement or surgical fixation of poorly positioned or dislocated intraocular lenses.

Procedures & Treatments

What Dr Wong Performs

Surgical Procedures

  • Pars Plana Vitrectomy — microsurgical removal of vitreous to treat retinal and vitreous pathology
  • Scleral Buckling — external surgical technique to repair retinal detachment
  • ILM Peeling — removal of the internal limiting membrane for macular hole and epiretinal membrane surgery
  • Scleral Fixation of IOLs — including sutureless flanged intrascleral fixation (Yamane technique)
  • IOL Exchange Surgery — removal and replacement of intraocular lenses
  • Cryotherapy — cryogenic freezing used in retinal detachment surgery

Injection-Based Treatments

Dr Wong performs intravitreal injections of anti-VEGF agents — including ranibizumab, aflibercept, brolucizumab, and faricimab — for wet macular degeneration, diabetic macular oedema, retinal vein occlusion, and other neovascular conditions, as well as corticosteroid injections for inflammatory retinal conditions.

Laser-Based Treatments

  • Laser Retinopexy — sealing retinal tears before they progress to detachment
  • Photocoagulation — laser treatment for retinal breaks and diabetic retinopathy
  • Panretinal Photocoagulation (PRP) — for advanced diabetic retinopathy and retinal vein occlusion

Published Research

Contributions to the Medical Literature

Dr Chee Wai Wong has published extensively in peer-reviewed international journals on topics spanning vitreoretinal surgery, high myopia, macular disease, diabetic eye disease, and drug delivery for retinal conditions. His research has been cited widely and covers surgical outcomes, diagnostic approaches, and treatment strategies for complex retinal pathology.

He has contributed to landmark studies on retinal detachment outcomes, myopic macular degeneration progression, geographic atrophy, and AI-based imaging for retinal disease — sharing findings with the global ophthalmology community.

Frequently Asked Questions

About Dr Wong

Where does Dr Chee Wai Wong practise?

Dr Chee Wai Wong practises at Asia Pacific Eye Centre, located at Gleneagles Hospital, 6A Napier Road, Singapore 258500. Gleneagles Hospital is a leading private hospital providing comprehensive ophthalmology services.

What conditions does Dr Wong treat?

Dr Wong specialises in vitreoretinal disease and treats a wide range of retinal and macular conditions, including retinal detachment, age-related macular degeneration (AMD), diabetic retinopathy, macular holes, retinal vein occlusion, high myopia complications, eye floaters, dislocated intraocular lenses, and epiretinal membranes. He also manages complications arising from previous eye surgery.

How many years of experience does Dr Wong have?

Dr Chee Wai Wong has over 15 years of experience as an ophthalmologist, having practised since 2009. During this time, he has developed substantial expertise in vitreoretinal surgery and the management of complex retinal diseases.

Does Dr Wong perform intravitreal injections?

Yes, Dr Wong regularly performs intravitreal injections as part of his clinical practice. These include anti-VEGF injections (ranibizumab, aflibercept, brolucizumab, faricimab) for wet macular degeneration, diabetic macular oedema, and retinal vein occlusion, as well as corticosteroid injections for macular oedema and inflammatory conditions.

What surgical procedures does Dr Wong perform?

Dr Wong performs a comprehensive range of retinal and vitreous surgery, including pars plana vitrectomy, scleral buckling, internal limiting membrane (ILM) peeling, retinal laser treatment, scleral fixation of intraocular lenses, IOL exchange surgery, and cryotherapy.
